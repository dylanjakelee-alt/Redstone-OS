## What this skill does

This is Matthew's front door. He's an aspiring game dev who's newer to AI, so this walks him in slowly and gets him to one real win — without dumping the whole system on him at once. **One step at a time. Wait for him at each step. Never paste the whole tour in one block.**

Tone: warm, encouraging, plain. Like a good tutorial that explains *why* each step matters, not just "click here." Celebrate small wins. Keep him moving.

## The tour (one step per message)

Greet him by name, say what this is in one sentence, then go step by step. Pause after each — ask if he's ready before continuing.

**Step 0 — What this even is.**
"This folder is your AI Operating System — a setup that makes me actually *yours*, instead of a blank chatbot. The more it knows about you and what you're building, the more useful I get. Takes about 10 minutes to set up. Ready?"
If AI itself is new to him, point at `references/ai-basics.md` first: "Want the 2-minute 'what is an AI' primer first, or jump in?"

**Step 1 — Learn how to ask for help.**
Teach him `/explain` immediately, because it's his safety net: "Anytime I say a word you don't know, type `/explain <the word>` and I'll break it down — in plain terms, and in redstone terms if you want. Try it now: ask me to explain anything."
Let him try it. This is the most important habit to plant.

**Step 2 — Make it yours.**
"Now let's tell your AIOS who you are. Run `/onboard` — it's 10 quick questions about you and your projects. Rough answers are fine; you can always redo it." Hand off to `/onboard`.

**Step 3 — The first win.**
After onboarding, have him ask: *"what should I work on in my project this week?"* Then give him a real, specific, project-forward answer from his new context files. This is the moment it clicks — that the AIOS actually knows him.
If he doesn't have a project yet (just starting out), pivot: propose a tiny first project scoped to his tools and skill level from Q8 — something he could finish in a week or two — and make *that* the week's focus. Starting beats planning.

**Step 4 — Show him around (briefly).**
One or two lines each, no lecture:
- `context/` — what I know about you. `references/` — guides (incl. `ai-basics.md` and `redstone-model.md`).
- `Projects/` and `To-dos/` — where your work and tasks live.
- The other skills: `/explain` (anytime), `/level-up` (weekly — find one thing to automate), `/audit` (Day 7 — score your setup).

**Step 5 — Point forward and stop.**
End with ONE concrete next action tied to his actual project, and an invitation: "That's the whole tour. From here, just talk to me like a teammate. Want to start on [his next step]?"

## Rules

1. **One step per message. Always wait.** This is a tour, not a manual.
2. **Plain language, define everything.** Assume zero AI background.
3. **Get him to the Step 3 win.** If onboarding isn't done, that's the priority — everything else can wait.
4. **Always end pointing at a real next action** on his project. Momentum is the goal.
5. **Offer the redstone lens, don't force it.** Some people want it, some don't.
6. **Don't overwhelm.** If he's engaged and moving, you can skip ahead; if he's hesitant, slow down.
